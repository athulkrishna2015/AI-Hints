# Effective learning: Twenty rules of formulating knowledge
*Dr Piotr Wozniak (February 1999)*

This document summarizes the core principles of formulating knowledge for spaced repetition, as utilized by the AI-Hints engine.

## Core Rules for AI Generation

1. **Do not learn if you do not understand**: Hints should bridge the gap of understanding, not just provide the answer.
2. **Learn before you memorize**: AI should provide context that builds a "big picture."
3. **Build upon the basics**: Use simple, foundational terminology.
4. **Stick to the minimum information principle**: Keep hints and MCQ options extremely short and specific.
5. **Cloze deletion is easy and effective**: For cloze cards, the AI must focus only on the missing part.
6. **Avoid sets and enumerations**: Do not generate lists. Focus on individual components.
7. **Combat interference**: Distractors should be similar enough to challenge the memory but distinct enough to prevent confusion.
8. **Optimize wording**: Eliminate all unnecessary words.
9. **Context cues simplify wording**: Use brief category tags (e.g., `[Bio]`, `[Law]`) to set the mental stage.
10. **Personalize and provide examples**: Use "vivid" or "concrete" examples when possible.
